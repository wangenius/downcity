## Design Context

### Users
- Primary users are operators, technical founders, and small engineering teams managing multiple repositories, clients, or business lines in parallel.
- They usually work under time pressure and need clear control over which agent is doing what, where context lives, and how to avoid cross-project confusion.
- The core job-to-be-done is to turn each folder/repo into a reliable, auditable AI agent workstation and operate many of them from one command center.

### Brand Personality
- Personality: operational, trustworthy, decisive.
- Emotional goals: confidence, clarity, and momentum.
- Voice and tone should feel like an "operator console" rather than a marketing brochure: concrete, direct, and outcome-oriented.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Visual direction: editorial + industrial control-plane.
- Keep light mode as the default, with strong hierarchy and deliberate asymmetry.
- Favor structured grids, restrained but confident accent color, and tactile panel treatments over generic SaaS cards.
- Anti-reference: avoid neon-dark AI clichés, generic gradient-heavy startup templates, and over-rounded glassmorphism.

### Design Principles
1. Boundary first: every major section should reinforce "one folder, one agent" and operational control.
2. Operator readability over decoration: prioritize scannable hierarchy, spacing rhythm, and clear action paths.
3. One signature motif: maintain a recognizable "control-plane" visual language (grids, rails, status chips, panel framing).
4. Motion with intent: subtle entrance and hover feedback only where it supports comprehension.
5. Mobile parity: preserve core message and primary actions on small screens without hiding critical content.
