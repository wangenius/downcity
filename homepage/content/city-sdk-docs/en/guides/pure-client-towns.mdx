---
title: Pure-Client Towns
description: How new AI towns can start as frontend-first towns.
---

“Pure-client town” does not mean “no backend at all.” It means you stop rebuilding the AI backend for every town.

Downcity lets a new city start from the frontend, extension, or app and connect to an already deployed City. You can validate the town UX first, then decide how much town-specific backend you really need.

## Good fits

- lightweight web tools
- Chrome extensions
- desktop shells
- mobile entries
- client demos
- internal tools

## Recommended boundary

The client owns:

- UI and interaction
- calling `User City`
- holding `town_id`
- storing or refreshing `user_token`

City owns:

- provider keys
- model directory
- `user_token` verification
- service routing
- usage records
- hooks and billing logic

## The most common minimum shape

```plaintext
Town frontend
  ↓
Your login / token-issuing endpoint
  ↓
Downcity City
```

That town backend can stay very thin at first:

- user login
- `Admin City.tokens.apply()`
- returning `user_token + town_id` to the client

## Why this fits early towns better

- you stop copying provider integrations for every AI idea
- the frontend can ship much earlier
- multiple towns share one AI calling, logging, and quota layer

If the town gets more complex later, then add more business backend. Do not duplicate the whole AI infrastructure on day one.
