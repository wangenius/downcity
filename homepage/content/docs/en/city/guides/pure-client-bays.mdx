---
title: Pure-Client Bays
description: How new AI bays can start as frontend-first bays.
---

“Pure-client bay” does not mean “no backend at all.” It means you stop rebuilding the AI backend for every bay.

Downcity lets a new city start from the frontend, extension, or app and connect to an already deployed City. You can validate the bay UX first, then decide how much bay-specific backend you really need.

## Good fits

- lightweight web tools
- Chrome extensions
- desktop shells
- mobile entries
- client demos
- internal tools

## Recommended boundary

The client owns:

- UI and interaction
- calling `User Gate`
- holding `bay_id`
- storing or refreshing `user_token`

City owns:

- provider keys
- model directory
- `user_token` verification
- service routing
- usage records
- hooks and billing logic

## The most common minimum shape

```plaintext
Bay frontend
  ↓
Your login / token-issuing endpoint
  ↓
Downcity City
```

That bay backend can stay very thin at first:

- user login
- `Admin Gate.tokens.apply()`
- returning `user_token + bay_id` to the client

## Why this fits early bays better

- you stop copying provider integrations for every AI idea
- the frontend can ship much earlier
- multiple bays share one AI calling, logging, and quota layer

If the bay gets more complex later, then add more business backend. Do not duplicate the whole AI infrastructure on day one.
