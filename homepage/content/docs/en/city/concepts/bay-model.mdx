title: Bay Model

The core mental model of Downcity is not "one bay, one backend." It is:

> one long-running City serving many AI bays.

The reason is simple. What gets rebuilt again and again is usually not your business backend, but the AI call layer:

- provider key storage
- model catalog
- token validation
- usage records
- hooks, quotas, billing, and logs

Once you ship a second or third AI bay, these capabilities repeat everywhere.

## What should stay in City

- Model catalog and provider configuration
- `user_token` validation
- AI service calls such as `client.ai.text()`, `client.ai.stream()`, and `client.ai.image()`
- Usage records, logs, quotas, and business hooks
- Shared call logic reused across multiple bays

## What should stay in the bay or business system

- User registration, login, and account systems
- Orders, subscriptions, balances, and CRM
- Pages, interactions, workflows, and content data
- Each bay's own brand, pricing, and go-to-market strategy

In other words, Downcity centralizes the **AI infrastructure** layer, not your whole business system.

## Why this is more stable than "one backend per project"

If every new bay copies a separate AI backend, the same issues appear quickly:

- provider keys scattered across many places
- inconsistent token rules
- inconsistent usage and logging conventions
- every new provider, model, or quota strategy requires edits in many repos

Once the AI call layer is centralized into one City, a new bay usually only needs two steps:

1. create a new `bay_id`
2. connect `User Visa` in the bay

## What `bay_id` means in this model

`bay_id` is not a model name, and it is not the full abstraction of your tenant system.

It is closer to City's primary key for the "bay boundary." It answers questions like:

- which city started this call
- whether this bay is currently `active`
- which bay dimension hooks and usage should record against
- whether a user's calls should be separated across different bays

If you are building a multi-bay portfolio, `bay_id` is the most stable boundary.

## The role of model at the bay layer

In the current implementation, a model is a runtime capability unit registered in Runtime, not something owned by a specific bay.

At the bay layer, a model is more like a visible capability:

- you can pass it explicitly into `client.ai.text({ model })`
- or omit it and let AIService use the default model for the current pathway

This means the bay sees stable model IDs, not upstream provider details.
