Downcity is a shared AI infrastructure runtime for AI towns. It gives you one long-running City that multiple web apps, extensions, apps, desktop tools, client demos, and internal tools can reuse.

What City exposes to towns is not a raw provider API. It exposes a stable **town-facing calling boundary**:

- the town holds `town_id + user_token`
- it calls `text()`, `stream()`, `image()`, and other services through `User City`
- City keeps the model directory, provider routing, hooks, usage, and env behind the boundary

## What City owns

- runtime database and Service-owned tables
- provider keys and runtime env
- `user_token` verification and `admin_secret_key` management
- model metadata and handlers through `Provider.model()` + `AIService.use()`
- public model catalogs through `client.ai.listModels()`
- usage records and hook extension points

## What City does not own

- your user system
- orders, subscriptions, CRM, or content data
- town-specific pages, interaction, and business UX
- a public “unified provider API” for arbitrary third-party systems

More precisely:

> City owns the shared AI infrastructure layer.
> Towns still own their users, business logic, interface, and pricing model.
